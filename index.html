A No bullsh*t 28 days guide to Improve Critical Thinking, Writing, and Speaking.

“If You Can Think and Speak and Write, You Are Absolutely Deadly.”
- Jordan Peterson

Week 1: Foundation Building

Day 1

  • Critical Thinking (15 min): Read a short article (e.g., BBC News). Identify argument, evidence, biases. Write 3 questions.
  • Writing (20 min): Write a 200-word summary of the article with your take. Focus on clarity.
  • Speaking (15 min): Practice delivering the summary aloud. Speak slowly and clearly.

Day 2

  • Critical Thinking (10 min): Solve a logic puzzle (e.g., Braingle). Note your approach.
  • Writing (20 min): Write a 300-word reflection on a personal experience. Use structure.
  • Speaking (15 min): Practice a 2-min talk on your reflection. Use gestures.

Day 3

  • Critical Thinking (15 min): Watch a TED talk (5-10 min). Spot main points and evidence.
  • Writing (20 min): Write a 250-word critique of the talk’s strengths/weaknesses.
  • Speaking (15 min): Record a 3-min summary with critique. Vary tone.

Day 4

  • Critical Thinking (10 min): Find a social media post you disagree with. Write a counterargument.
  • Writing (15 min): Write a 200-word response explaining your view.
  • Speaking (15 min): Deliver response as a 2-min talk. Sound confident.

Day 5

  • Overlap (45 min): Pick an article. Analyze (15 min), write a 250-word summary (15 min), practice a 3-min talk (15 min).

Day 6

  • Critical Thinking (15 min): Read two articles on same topic, different views. Compare arguments.
  • Writing (20 min): Write 300 words on which argument is stronger.
  • Speaking (15 min): Deliver a 3-min talk on your conclusion. Smooth transitions.

Day 7: Reflection

  • Reflection (20 min): Review writings/talks. Note improvements. Set goals for Week 2.

Week 2: Skill Deepening

Day 8

  • Critical Thinking (10 min): Read a news article. Identify key points.
  • Writing (20 min): Write a 300-word opinion piece on the article.
  • Speaking (15 min): Deliver a 3-min talk on your opinion. Practice eye contact.

Day 9

  • Critical Thinking (10 min): Solve an ethical dilemma (e.g., self-driving cars).
  • Writing (20 min): Write a 350-word reflection on your conclusion.
  • Speaking (15 min): Practice a 3-min talk explaining it. Use storytelling.

Day 10

  • Overlap (50 min): Watch a 10-min podcast/video. Write a summary (15 min), practice a talk (15 min).

Day 11

  • Critical Thinking (15 min): Analyze a controversial piece. Spot fallacies.
  • Writing (20 min): Write a 300-word rebuttal.
  • Speaking (15 min): Deliver rebuttal as a 3-min talk. Be assertive.

Day 12

  • Writing (25 min): Write a 400-word persuasive essay on a topic you care about.
  • Speaking (20 min): Deliver essay as a 4-min talk. Focus on pacing.

Day 13

  • Critical Thinking (15 min): Debate a friend on a light topic.
  • Writing (15 min): Write a 200-word summary of the debate.
  • Speaking (15 min): Record a 2-min reflection.

Day 14: Reflection

  • Reflection (20 min): Compare Week 2 to Week 1. Set goals for Week 3.

Week 3: Integration and Application

Day 15

  • Combined (45 min): Pick a passion topic. Research (15 min), write 400 words (15 min), practice a 4-min talk (15 min).

Day 16

  • Critical Thinking (15 min): Watch a speech/debate clip. Identify techniques/flaws.
  • Writing (20 min): Write a 300-word analysis of its effectiveness.
  • Speaking (15 min): Deliver a 3-min talk on your analysis.

Day 17

  • Overlap (50 min): Read an article (10 min), write a 350-word summary (20 min), practice a 4-min talk (20 min).

Day 18

  • Speaking (20 min): Record a 5-min talk on a researched topic.
  • Writing (15 min): Write a 250-word self-critique.
  • Critical Thinking (10 min): Reflect on how thinking shaped your talk.

Day 19

  • Critical Thinking (15 min): Debate someone on a current issue.
  • Writing (20 min): Write a 300-word summary and takeaways.
  • Speaking (15 min): Practice a 3-min talk on your summary.

Day 20

  • Writing (25 min): Write a 500-word article on a topic of choice.
  • Speaking (20 min): Practice key points as a 5-min talk.

Day 21: Reflection

  • Reflection (20 min): Review Week 3. Note integration gains. Set goals for Week 4.

Week 4: Refinement and Assessment

Day 22

  • Writing (20 min): Revise a Week 1 piece for clarity and impact.
  • Speaking (15 min): Re-record a Week 1 talk. Compare improvements.
  • Critical Thinking (10 min): Reflect on thinking gains with examples.

Day 23

  • Critical Thinking (15 min): Analyze an ethical dilemma (e.g., AI in hiring).
  • Writing (20 min): Write a 400-word piece on your stance.
  • Speaking (15 min): Deliver a 4-min talk on your stance.

Day 24

  • Overlap (50 min): Pick a topic. Analyze (15 min), write 400 words (20 min), practice a talk (15 min).

Day 25

  • Speaking (20 min): Give a 5-min talk to someone. Get feedback.
  • Writing (15 min): Write a 250-word reflection on feedback.
  • Critical Thinking (10 min): Analyze feedback vs. self-assessment.

Day 26

  • Writing (25 min): Write a 500-word piece on a past struggle topic.
  • Speaking (20 min): Practice a 5-min talk with engagement.

Day 27

  • Critical Thinking (15 min): Solve a tough logic puzzle.
  • Writing (20 min): Write a 300-word piece on your solution.
  • Speaking (15 min): Deliver a 3-min talk on your approach.

Day 28: Final Assessment

  • Critical Thinking (15 min): Analyze a complex article/dilemma.
  • Writing (25 min): Write a 500-word piece on it.
  • Speaking (20 min): Deliver a 5-min talk. Record and review.
