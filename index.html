A No bullsh*t 28 days guide to Improve Thinking, Writing, and Speaking

This is a 28-day plan to improve your critical thinking, writing, and speaking skills . Each day, you will have tasks to complete in each of these areas. The tasks are designed to build on each other and help you develop these skills over time. By the end of the 28 days, you should notice a significant improvement in your ability to think critically, write clearly, and speak confidently.

“If You Can Think and Speak and Write, You Are Absolutely Deadly.”
- Jordan Peterson

4 Week Action Plan

Week 1: Foundation Building (click to see more)

Day 1

  • Thinking: Think about a topic of interest and write down 5 questions you have about it. Consider how you might find answers.
  • Writing: Brainstorm and select a problem or topic to write about. Write a brief statement explaining why it’s important to you.
  • Speaking: Practice speaking aloud, focusing on clarity and pronunciation. Read a passage from a book or newspaper and record yourself.

Day 2

  • Thinking: Identify a problem that interests you and write down why it grips you. Think about initial steps to engage with it.
  • Writing: Find and read at least one article or chapter related to your chosen problem. Take notes on key insights.
  • Speaking: Record yourself speaking about a topic for 5 minutes. Listen and note areas for improvement.

Day 3

  • Thinking: Imagine a conversation between two viewpoints on a topic. Write down arguments from each side.
  • Writing: Write a paragraph on any topic, then rewrite it for clarity and structure. Reflect on the process.
  • Speaking: Practice speaking in formal and casual styles on the same topic. Record and note differences.

Day 4

  • Thinking: Reflect on a recent conversation where you might have been too confident. Think about how to approach it with humility.
  • Writing: Write a 300-word piece on a personal experience, focusing on your unique voice.
  • Speaking: Craft an attention-grabbing opening for a 2-minute speech. Practice delivering it.

Day 5

  • Thinking: Identify a strong belief and write reasons why you might be wrong. Consider alternative perspectives.
  • Writing: Revise a previous piece, focusing on structure and eliminating unnecessary words.
  • Speaking: Prepare a short speech on a topic you feel strongly about. Record and review.

Day 6

  • Thinking: Engage in a discussion on a topic you care about. Reflect on what you learned.
  • Writing: Take a paragraph and rearrange sentences for better flow.
  • Speaking: Listen to a podcast or read a book, rating its articulacy on a scale of 1-10.

Day 7

  • Thinking: Read an article or watch a video with a viewpoint opposite to yours. Write your reactions.
  • Writing: Summarize a longer piece or a book chapter into a ten-sentence outline.
  • Speaking: Spend 5 minutes reading, 5 minutes reading poetry aloud, and 5 minutes listening to articulate speech.

Week 2: Intermediate Skills (click to see more)

Day 8

  • Thinking: Map out a system in your life (e.g., workplace) and analyze how its parts interact.
  • Writing: Write a 300-word argumentative paragraph with a clear claim, evidence, and reasoning.
  • Speaking: Prepare a 3-minute speech with two audience questions. Practice delivery.

Day 9

  • Thinking: Research one cognitive bias (e.g., confirmation bias) and write how to mitigate it.
  • Writing: Add two pieces of evidence to your argumentative paragraph from Day 8.
  • Speaking: Craft a 2-minute personal story related to a topic. Practice telling it aloud.

Day 10

  • Thinking: Use a pros and cons list for a decision you're facing. Write your thought process.
  • Writing: Write a 300-word piece on a passionate topic, focusing on your style.
  • Speaking: Practice a short speech after a 5-minute relaxation exercise. Note the effect.

Day 11

  • Thinking: Think of three unconventional solutions to a problem. Evaluate their feasibility.
  • Writing: Revise a piece to remove redundant words and simplify sentences.
  • Speaking: Prepare a 2-minute speech with deliberate pauses. Record and review.

Day 12

  • Thinking: Formulate questions on a topic and try to answer them.
  • Writing: Write a 300-word piece with transitional words to link ideas.
  • Speaking: Prepare two 2-minute speeches: one for a general audience, one for experts. Note differences.

Day 13

  • Thinking: Practice mindfulness by focusing on your breath or an object. Note its effect on concentration.
  • Writing: Write a short piece in an unfamiliar genre (e.g., poetry). Reflect on the experience.
  • Speaking: Create a simple visual aid for a 3-minute speech. Practice integrating it.

Day 14

  • Thinking: Research one logical fallacy (e.g., ad hominem). Write examples and how to avoid it.
  • Writing: Share a piece with someone and ask for feedback on clarity, structure, and style.
  • Speaking: Choose a random topic and deliver a 2-minute impromptu speech. Record and review.

Week 3: Advanced Techniques (click to see more)

Day 15

  • Thinking: Reflect on a recent decision. Write down your thought process and evaluate its effectiveness.
  • Writing: Write a 500-word essay with a clear introduction, body, and conclusion.
  • Speaking: Prepare a 3-minute speech with two rhetorical devices (e.g., metaphors). Record and review.

Day 16

  • Thinking: Analyze a moral dilemma and write how you would resolve it.
  • Writing: Write a 300-word persuasive piece using pathos, logos, or ethos.
  • Speaking: Anticipate three difficult questions on a topic and practice answering them.

Day 17

  • Thinking: Use brainstorming or mind mapping to generate five creative solutions to a problem.
  • Writing: Revise a piece for style, experimenting with word choice and tone.
  • Speaking: Prepare a 3-minute speech with a clear call to action. Practice with passion.

Day 18

  • Thinking: Consider opposing viewpoints on a topic and find a synthesis.
  • Writing: Write a short story or scene using a technique like flashbacks.
  • Speaking: Prepare a 2-minute speech with a humorous anecdote. Practice delivery.

Day 19

  • Thinking: Research cognitive load theory and write how to apply it to your learning.
  • Writing: Thoroughly revise a piece, focusing on content and style.
  • Speaking: Read a text with different pitches, tones, and paces. Record and review.

Day 20

  • Thinking: Reflect on a recent interaction, analyzing emotions and how to improve emotional intelligence.
  • Writing: Write a 300-word piece tailored to a specific audience’s needs or concerns.
  • Speaking: Craft a 3-minute story from a personal experience. Practice telling it aloud.

Day 21

  • Thinking: Imagine three scenarios (best, worst, likely) for a goal and plan responses.
  • Writing: Write a 300-word piece explaining a complex concept with a metaphor or analogy.
  • Speaking: Prepare answers for five potential questions on a topic. Practice aloud.

Week 4: Integration and Refinement (click to see more)

Day 22

  • Thinking: Reflect on your 28-day journey. Write about what you’ve learned and how you’ve grown.
  • Writing: Choose a piece and prepare it for sharing (e.g., blog, social media).
  • Speaking: Research and list three public speaking opportunities. Plan to participate in one.

Day 23

  • Thinking: Identify a new skill or topic to learn and create a plan to start.
  • Writing: Share a piece and ask for detailed feedback. Revise based on comments.
  • Speaking: Record a practice speech and analyze strengths and weaknesses.

Day 24

  • Thinking: Prepare a short lesson on a concept you’ve learned and teach it to someone.
  • Writing: Write a short piece in an unfamiliar format (e.g., script, poem).
  • Speaking: Think about how to mentor someone in speaking (e.g., feedback, practice).

Day 25

  • Thinking: Write down things you’re grateful for and reflect on their impact.
  • Writing: Write a 500-word reflection on your 28-day journey.
  • Speaking: Prepare a 3-minute speech on a passionate topic. Focus on enthusiasm.

Day 26

  • Thinking: Read about a philosophical concept and write your thoughts.
  • Writing: Write a 300-word essay on a philosophical question.
  • Speaking: Prepare arguments for both sides of a philosophical question. Practice debating.

Day 27

  • Thinking: Visualize a goal and the steps to achieve it. Write down your visualization.
  • Writing: Write a personal vision statement for the next year.
  • Speaking: Prepare and practice a 3-minute speech about your vision.

Day 28

  • Thinking: Reflect on how thinking, writing, and speaking are interconnected. Write about your growth.
  • Writing: Write a 500-word summary of your 28-day experience.
  • Speaking: Prepare and deliver a 5-minute speech on what you’ve learned. Record and celebrate.
