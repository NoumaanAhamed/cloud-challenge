Master Financial Markets Through Learning, Analysis, Writing, and Communication

A comprehensive 28-day program designed to transform you into a confident market analyst. Through structured learning of economic principles, critical analysis of market movements, written insights, and articulate communication, you'll develop the skills to understand supply & demand, macroeconomics, market psychology, and geopolitical impacts on financial markets.

4 Week Action Plan

Week 1: Market Fundamentals (click to see more)

Day 1

  • Learning: Principle: Supply & Demand (15 min) - Watch a 15-min video on the fundamentals of Supply and Demand. This is the master principle.
  • Thinking: Find S/D 'Spark' (15 min) - Find one news headline where an asset (stock/crypto) moved > 5%. Ask: 'What did this news do to the supply? What did it do to the demand?'
  • Writing: 1-Paragraph Analyst Note (20 min) - Write one paragraph: 'Today, [Asset] moved [X%]. The news was [Spark]. This likely happened because it [increased/decreased] [supply/demand] by [explain reason].'
  • Speaking: 60-Second S/D Memo (10 min) - Record a 1-min voice note explaining your written paragraph, as if to a friend. Use simple words like 'want' (demand) and 'available' (supply).

Day 2

  • Learning: Principle: Incentives (15 min) - Read a short article on 'economic incentives'. Understand that all market participants (buyers, sellers, companies) act based on incentives.
  • Thinking: Find 'Incentive' Spark (15 min) - Find a headline about a company's decision (e.g., 'Tesla cuts prices,' 'Apple announces buyback'). Ask: 'What is the incentive for them to do this? How does this incentivize customers/investors?'
  • Writing: Incentive List (20 min) - In your journal, create two lists for your 'Spark': 1) 'Incentives for the company to do this:' 2) 'Incentives for customers/investors to react:'
  • Speaking: Explain the 'Why' (10 min) - Record a 1-min voice note. Don't state the news. Just explain the incentives behind it. 'Here's why [Company] did [X]... They are incentivized by...'

Day 3

  • Learning: Assets: Stock vs. Crypto (20 min) - Watch one 10-min video explaining 'What is a stock?' and another 10-min video on 'What is cryptocurrency? (e.g., Bitcoin)'. Focus on what gives them value.
  • Thinking: Compare S/D (15 min) - Think about Bitcoin vs. Apple stock. How is the supply of each determined? What factors drive demand for each? Are they different?
  • Writing: Venn Diagram / T-Chart (15 min) - In your journal, draw a T-chart or Venn diagram comparing the Supply/Demand factors for Bitcoin vs. a stock like Apple. Write 2-3 points for each.
  • Speaking: Explain the Difference (10 min) - Record a 2-min memo explaining the key difference in what drives value for a stock versus a cryptocurrency, based on your T-chart.

Day 4

  • Learning: Mechanics: Order Books (15 min) - Watch a 15-min video on 'how an exchange order book works'. Understand 'bid', 'ask', and 'market orders'. This is the engine of S/D.
  • Thinking: Find a 'Volume' Spark (15 min) - Find a headline that mentions 'high trading volume'. Ask: 'What does high volume mean in the context of an order book? Does it mean lots of buyers, sellers, or both?'
  • Writing: Predictive Journal Entry (20 min) - Find a new Spark. Before checking the price chart, write a prediction: 'The news is [Spark]. I believe this will [increase/decrease] [demand/supply]. Therefore, the price should [go up/down].'
  • Speaking: Speak Your Prediction (10 min) - Record a 1-min memo before you check the price. State your prediction and your 'why' clearly. Save it.

Day 5

  • Thinking: Review Prediction (15 min) - Listen to yesterday's memo. Now look at the chart. Were you right or wrong? Why? What did you miss? This feedback loop is critical.
  • Writing: Prediction Review (20 min) - In your journal, under yesterday's entry, write a 'Review:' paragraph. 'My prediction was [correct/wrong]. I was right/wrong because... The key factor I missed was...'
  • Speaking: Explain Your Mistake (or Win) (15 min) - Record a 2-min memo. The topic is 'What I learned from my last prediction.' Be honest and analytical about your thought process.
  • Learning: Passive Review (10 min) - Just browse financial news for 10 minutes. See if you can spot the S/D and incentive language in the articles.

Day 6

  • Learning: S/D Deep Dive: 'Elasticity' (15 min) - Watch a 10-min video on 'price elasticity of demand'. Ask: 'Is the demand for Bitcoin 'elastic' or 'inelastic'? What about gasoline?'
  • Thinking: Find 'Elasticity' Spark (15 min) - Find news about a price change for a common good (like gas, coffee, or a new phone). Ask: 'Will this price change cause a big drop in demand (elastic) or a small one (inelastic)? Why?'
  • Writing: Elastic/Inelastic Note (20 min) - Write a short note: '[Product] prices are changing. I think the demand is [elastic/inelastic] because... This means the company's total revenue will likely [go up/go down].'
  • Speaking: Explain Elasticity (10 min) - Record a 1-min memo explaining your 'elastic' or 'inelastic' call as if to a fellow analyst.

Day 7

  • Thinking: Weekly Review (20 min) - Read through your entire journal for Week 1. What was the most surprising connection you found? What are you still most confused about?
  • Writing: Week 1 Summary (20 min) - Write a 3-bullet-point summary: 'My 3 Key Takeaways on Supply & Demand'. 2. 'My Biggest Question Remaining.'
  • Speaking: Weekly Summary Memo (20 min) - Record a 3-minute memo summarizing your 3 takeaways and your biggest question. This solidifies the week's learning.

Week 2: Macroeconomics & Markets (click to see more)

Day 8

  • Learning: Macro Principle: 'The Price of Money' (20 min) - Learn: Interest Rates (cost of borrowing), Central Banks (e.g., US Fed, RBI - they set the rates), Inflation (prices rising). Watch a 20-min explainer on how these 3 interact.
  • Thinking: Find Macro 'Spark' (15 min) - Find the latest news on an interest rate decision or inflation report (CPI) from the US or India.
  • Writing: Start the 'Chain of Logic' (15 min) - In your journal, start the chain: '1. News: [e.g., Inflation is high]. 2. This means Central Bank will [raise rates]. 3. Why? [To slow spending]. 4. So what...'
  • Speaking: Practice the Chain (10 min) - Record a 1-min memo trying to speak out your 'Chain of Logic' so far. This will reveal gaps in your understanding.

Day 9

  • Learning: Macro Principle: GDP (10 min) - Watch a 10-min video on 'What is GDP (Gross Domestic Product)?'. Understand what it means when it's 'growing' or 'shrinking'.
  • Thinking: Find GDP 'Spark' (15 min) - Find your country's (or the US's) latest GDP report. Was it higher or lower than expected? What does that imply about the economy's health?
  • Writing: GDP 'So What?' Note (20 min) - Write a paragraph: 'The latest GDP report showed [growth/shrinkage]. This was [above/below] expectations. This suggests the economy is [hot/cold], which means the Central Bank might be [more/less] likely to [raise/lower] rates.'
  • Speaking: Explain GDP News (15 min) - Record a 1-min memo explaining why the 'boring' GDP number matters, connecting it to the Central Bank and interest rates.

Day 10

  • Thinking: Connect Week 1 & 2 (20 min) - The key connection: How do interest rates (Week 2) affect the demand for assets (Week 1)? Hint: If a 'safe' bank account pays 5%, are you as 'demanding' of a risky stock?
  • Writing: The 'Gravity' Note (25 min) - Write a paragraph explaining this connection. Title it 'Interest Rates are Gravity.' (e.g., 'Higher rates pull money out of risky assets... this decreases demand for stocks/crypto, so prices fall.')
  • Speaking: Explain 'Gravity' (15 min) - Record a 2-min memo explaining the 'Rates as Gravity' analogy to a friend. This is a core concept.

Day 11

  • Thinking: Find New Macro Spark (15 min) - Find any new macro (inflation, rates, GDP) headline. This time, predict the market reaction for both stocks and crypto.
  • Writing: Macro Prediction Journal (25 min) - Write a new prediction entry: 'Event: [Macro Spark]. My Chain: [e.g., News -> Rates -> Demand]. Prediction (Stocks): [Down]. Prediction (Crypto): [Down].'
  • Speaking: The 'Dinner Table' Pitch (20 min) - Record a 2-min memo explaining your prediction. Goal: Make it a clear, compelling story. 'Here's what just happened, and here's why it means [X]...'

Day 12

  • Thinking: Review Prediction (20 min) - Check yesterday's prediction against the market. Were you right/wrong? Why? Did the market do the opposite? (This happens! Why?)
  • Writing: Prediction Review (20 min) - Write your review: 'My prediction was [correct/wrong]. The market [did/didn't] do what I expected. I think this is because [e.g., the news was already 'priced in', or I missed...].'
  • Speaking: Explain 'Priced In' (20 min) - Record a 1-min memo on the concept of news being 'priced in' (i.e., the market already expected it, so there was no reaction).

Day 13

  • Learning: Podcast: 'Planet Money' (30 min) - Listen to one 20-30 minute episode of 'Planet Money' or 'The Indicator' podcast related to a macro topic. This is active, on-the-go learning.
  • Writing: Podcast Summary (20 min) - Write 3 bullet points summarizing the podcast episode. 1) What was the main topic? 2) What was the key insight? 3) What is one new question you have?
  • Speaking: Podcast Insight (10 min) - Record a 1-min memo on the 'key insight' you just wrote down.

Day 14

  • Thinking: Weekly Review (20 min) - Read your Week 2 journal. What is the clearest connection between Macro (rates) and Markets (S/D)? What is the muddiest?
  • Writing: Week 2 Summary (20 min) - Write 3 bullet points: 'My 3 Key Takeaways on Macroeconomics'. 2. 'My Biggest Question Remaining.'
  • Speaking: Weekly Summary Memo (20 min) - Record a 3-minute memo summarizing your 3 takeaways and your biggest question. This solidifies the week's learning.

Week 3: Market Psychology & Valuation (click to see more)

Day 15

  • Learning: Psychology: 'Risk-On/Risk-Off' (15 min) - Learn this concept. Risk-On: Investors feel 'greedy' (buy stocks/crypto). Risk-Off: Investors feel 'fearful' (sell risk, buy 'safe' bonds/cash). Watch a 15-min video.
  • Thinking: Connect Macro & Psychology (15 min) - How does macro news cause a 'Risk-Off' mood? (e.g., 'Bad inflation report -> Fed raises rates -> Fear of recession -> Risk-Off!'). Trace this.
  • Writing: Find the 'Mood' (20 min) - Look at the market today (e.g., S&P 500 or Bitcoin). Is it a 'Risk-On' or 'Risk-Off' day? Find one headline and write: 'The market feels [Risk-On/Off]. I think this is driven by [News Spark].'
  • Speaking: Explain the 'Mood' (10 min) - Record a 1-min memo: 'Today, the market sentiment is [mood], and the main driver seems to be...'

Day 16

  • Learning: Psychology: 'FOMO & FUD' (15 min) - Learn 'Fear Of Missing Out' (FOMO) and 'Fear, Uncertainty, Doubt' (FUD). These drive bubbles and crashes.
  • Thinking: Find 'Emotion' Spark (15 min) - Find a headline about a meme stock or a crypto coin making a nonsensical 50%+ move. Ask: 'Is this FOMO or FUD?'
  • Writing: The 'Contrarian' Note (20 min) - Write a paragraph arguing against the crowd. 'Everyone is buying [Meme Coin] due to FOMO. This is irrational because [list 1-2 fundamental reasons]. This is likely a bubble.'
  • Speaking: The 'Contrarian' Argument (10 min) - Record a 2-min memo making your contrarian case. This practices independent thinking.

Day 17

  • Learning: Valuation: 'P/E Ratio' (20 min) - Learn what the 'Price-to-Earnings (P/E)' Ratio is. It's a basic (not perfect) check for 'cheap' vs. 'expensive'.
  • Thinking: Apply P/E (15 min) - Look up the P/E ratio for two competitors (e.g., Google vs. Microsoft, or Ford vs. Tesla). Which is 'cheaper'? Why might the 'expensive' one have a high P/E?
  • Writing: P/E Comparison (15 min) - Write a short note: '[Company A] has a P/E of [X] and [Company B] has a P/E of [Y]. [Company B] is 'more expensive' because the market expects it to grow faster. This is [psychology/fundamentals].'
  • Speaking: Explain P/E (10 min) - Record a 1-min memo explaining which company is 'cheaper' and why the other is more expensive.

Day 18

  • Thinking: Find 'Fundamentals vs. Psych' (15 min) - Find news about a stock (e.g., NVIDIA, Tesla). Ask: 'Is this news impacting its fundamentals (long-term profit) or just its psychology (short-term FOMO)?'
  • Writing: Fundamentals vs. Psych Journal (25 min) - Write a prediction: 'The news is [Spark]. I think this is 90% psychology / 10% fundamentals. Therefore, the price move [will/will not] last.'
  • Speaking: Argue Your Case (20 min) - Record a 2-min memo arguing your 'psychology vs. fundamentals' call. Justify your percentage breakdown.

Day 19

  • Learning: The 'Safe' Asset: Bonds (20 min) - Watch a 15-min video on 'What is a government bond?' and 'What is a 'yield'?'. This is the benchmark for 'Risk-Off'.
  • Thinking: The 'Yield' Connection (15 min) - Key concept: 'Why buy risky Bitcoin (0% yield) if a 'safe' government bond yields 5%?' How does the bond yield affect demand for crypto?
  • Writing: Bonds vs. Crypto Note (15 min) - Write a paragraph explaining the competition: 'As bond yields [rise], they become [more] attractive. This pulls money away from non-yielding assets like crypto and gold, [decreasing] their demand.'
  • Speaking: Explain the Competition (10 min) - Record a 1-min memo. Start with: 'If I offered you a 5% safe return, would you still gamble...'

Day 20

  • Thinking: Prediction Review (20 min) - Review all your predictions from Weeks 1-3. Where are you consistently wrong? Are you too optimistic (FOMO)? Too pessimistic (FUD)?
  • Writing: Identify Your 'Bias' (20 min) - Write a paragraph: 'My biggest bias so far seems to be... I tend to [overestimate/underestimate] the impact of [e.g., psychology, rates].'
  • Speaking: Confess Your Bias (20 min) - Record a 2-min memo acknowledging your bias and stating one way you'll try to correct for it next week.

Day 21

  • Thinking: Weekly Review (20 min) - Read your Week 3 journal. How does 'emotion' interact with the 'logic' of Week 1 and 2? Which one seems to win more often in the short term?
  • Writing: Week 3 Summary (20 min) - Write 3 bullet points: 'My 3 Key Takeaways on Market Psychology'. 2. 'My Biggest Question Remaining.'
  • Speaking: Weekly Summary Memo (20 min) - Record a 3-minute memo summarizing your 3 takeaways and your biggest question. This solidifies the week's learning.

Week 4: Synthesis & Mastery (click to see more)

Day 22

  • Learning: Synthesis: Geopolitics & Oil (20 min) - Learn how Oil is the 'master commodity' (it's an input cost for everything). Learn how Geopolitics (conflicts, alliances) affects oil supply.
  • Thinking: Find 'Geopolitical' Spark (10 min) - Find a headline about OPEC, a conflict in the Middle East, or oil prices.
  • Writing: Start the 'Full Chain' Map (30 min) - In your journal, trace the entire chain. '1. Event: [OPEC cuts supply]. 2. Impact: Oil supply down (Wk 1). 3. Consequence: Gas prices up. 4. Macro: This is inflation (Wk 2). 5. CB Reaction: ...'

Day 23

  • Thinking: Complete the 'Full Chain' (15 min) - Finish yesterday's chain map. Connect it all the way to Week 3. '...CB raises rates -> Fear of recession -> 'Risk-Off' mood (Wk 3) -> People sell stocks/crypto.'
  • Writing: Write the 'Full Chain' Story (25 min) - Turn your chain map into a 2-paragraph story. 'It started with an OPEC decision, but it ended with the crypto market falling. Here's how...'
  • Speaking: Speak the 'Full Chain' (20 min) - Record a 2-min memo telling this story. This is the ultimate synthesis test.

Day 24

  • Learning: Synthesis: Regulation & Tariffs (15 min) - Learn how new laws, regulations, or tariffs (taxes on imports) act as new incentives (Wk 1) for companies.
  • Thinking: Find 'Political' Spark (15 min) - Find a headline about a new law, a politician threatening regulation (e.g., on AI or crypto), or new tariffs.
  • Writing: Trace the 'Incentive' Chain (20 min) - Write a chain: '1. Event: [New tariff on solar panels]. 2. Incentive: [More expensive to import]. 3. Company Action: [Buy domestic, or raise prices]. 4. Market Impact: [Domestic solar stocks up, other prices up].'
  • Speaking: Explain the Political Impact (10 min) - Record a 2-min memo explaining how this political decision will economically affect companies and consumers.

Day 25

  • Thinking: Find Your 'Capstone' Spark (10 min) - Find the biggest financial/political news story of the day. It doesn't matter what it is.
  • Writing: The 'Capstone' Memo (35 min) - Write a 300-word memo titled 'Why [Spark] Matters.' Use all your principles: S/D (Wk 1), Macro (Wk 2), Psychology (Wk 3), and Political links (Wk 4). This is your 'final exam' for writing.
  • Speaking: Memo Read-Through (15 min) - Read your Capstone Memo out loud to yourself. This helps catch awkward phrasing and flawed logic. Make edits.

Day 26

  • Thinking: Refine the 'Capstone' (15 min) - Re-read yesterday's memo. Find the single most important sentence (the 'thesis'). Your speaking task will be built around this.
  • Speaking: The 60-Second 'CEO Briefing' (45 min) - This is your 'final exam' for speaking. Record a 60-second, high-energy brief of your memo. Format: 1. 'Here's what happened.' 2. 'Here's why it matters.' 3. 'Here's what I'm watching next.' Do it 5-10 times until it's perfect.

Day 27

  • Thinking: Full Journal Review (30 min) - Read your entire 4-week journal, from Day 1. Look at your first 'Spark' vs. your 'Capstone' memo. How has your thinking changed?
  • Writing: My 'Gaps & Gains' List (20 min) - Write two lists: 1. 'My 3 Biggest Gains' (e.g., 'I now understand rates'). 2. 'My 3 Biggest Gaps' (e.g., 'I still don't get bonds', 'My bias is...').
  • Speaking: Course Summary Memo (10 min) - Record a 3-min memo summarizing your 'Gaps & Gains'.

Day 28

  • Writing: My Ongoing 'Analyst's Loop' (30 min) - The course is over; the habit begins. Write a 1-page document defining your new sustainable routine. 'I will analyze [X] Sparks per week.' 'I will journal [X] times.' 'I will review my predictions every [Sunday].'
  • Speaking: My 'Mission Statement' (15 min) - Record a 3-minute 'mission statement' for your continued learning. 'My goal is to... I will achieve this by focusing on... I will avoid...'
  • Learning: Find Your Sources (15 min) - Final task: Find 3-5 high-quality analysts, podcasts, or newsletters to follow. Your learning is now about curation and application.
